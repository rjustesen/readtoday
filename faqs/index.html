What is Read Today?

Read Today is a high impact literacy program that motivates, educates and celebrates Utah’s kids. The Read Today team hosts and supports a variety of programs and events throughout the year. Read Today also has teamed up with AmeriCorps in their Read.Graduate.Succeed program which provides struggling readers with volunteer tutors in more than 120 Utah schools.


What is the purpose of Read Today?

The purpose of Read Today is to help Utah kids become proficient in reading, have access to books in their community and develop a love of literacy. Read Today also supports Governor Herbert's goal of having 90% of Utah students be proficient readers.


HOW DOES THE READ TODAY VOLUNTEER PROGRAM WORK?

AmeriCorps administers the program, uses the USOE's STAR program to train volunteers, collect data and ensure quality and consistency. KSL creates awareness, and provides incentives and celebrations with Chopper 5 landings and literacy events. Partners who share our goals include The United Way, Utah State Office of Education, PTA and the Chamber's Prosperity 2020.


WHAT KIND OF COMMITMENT IS ASKED OF A READING TUTOR?

Volunteers are asked to provide 1-2 hours of service a week at an elementary school in your area. You will be tutoring a child 1:1 in a public area, where other tutors are serving, with supervision of a school reading coordinator. Prior to tutoring, volunteers are given a brief training and taught how to properly help children learn how to read.


DOES READ TODAY OFFER A SUMMER READING PROGRAM?

Yes, every year, we feature a Summer Reading Calendar in a special edition of the Deseret News. To obtain your copy, check with your local school, or email contact@readtoday.com.


HOW CAN I GET CHOPPER 5 TO MY SCHOOL?

We are always interested in hearing how your school is doing great things. Send us your story to contact@readtoday.com. We believe in telling the story of the community. Let us know what you’re doing!


I AM A MEMBER OF PTA, HOW CAN I BE INVOLVED?

Reach out to your PTA president or Education Commissioner and see what opportunities your school has to help struggling readers. Or you can email contact@readtoday.com.
